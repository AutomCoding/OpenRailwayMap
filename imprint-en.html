Types of processed data

When using our services, the following information about your each of your requests to our services is save in our log files:

  • IP address
  • time of access
  • requested content (URL, query of your search request)
  • HTTP referrer (website linking to us)
  • user agent (name and version of your web browser and operating system)
  • HTTP status code The status code describes whether your request was successfully and allows conclusions to the reason.

These log files will be deleted after seven days.

If you are subscribed to our mailing list, the following data is stored about you as long as you are registered:

  • your name, if provided
  • your email address
  • date and time of registration
  • subscribed mailing lists
  • your OpenStreetMap user name if you used your OpenStreetMap account to sign up

If you send messages to our mailing list, the following data is stored about you permanently:

  • your name, if provided
  • your email address
  • date and time
  • content of the message

Mailing list archives are public. Your messages might be downloaded, stored and re-published by third parties.

If you send an email privately to us, we may save the sent message including your name and email address for two years.

Purpose of Processing, Legal Basis, Duration of Storage

In recording and processing this data, we are pursuing our legitimate interests as a data controller on the legal basis of Article 6 (1)(f) of the GDPR.

The collected data is used to detect and block excessive and incorrect usage of our services causing costs for us and limiting the usability by other users. If it is possible for us to determine the owner of the IP address, we might contact them. By contacting them, we would like to offer them the possibility to change their access patterns and avoid them to be blocked by us.

We use the data in aggregated form (number of accesses to certain pages or files) in order to determine the demand and adapt the service to it. Adding or removeing map styles or additional interfaces for searcing, routing or similar are common examples.

We might publish data in aggregated form. The data will be aggregated in a way to make it impossible to assign it to individual users. Examples for such publications are: number of access to certain regions on the map, map styles or pages as well as regions/IP subnets where the requests come from.

We will not use the data for marketing. We do not set cookies.

Log files are deleted after seven days.

We use data from communication by email in order to respond to your emails. In that case, our legal basis s article 6 (1)(b) GDPR. Archives might be kept for longer than two years if we are legally required to do so.

We do not share information with anyone else except legally required
